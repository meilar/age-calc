We will need you to provide us with your expected lifespan. We could ask you about your lifestyle habits, and apply your answers to actuarial formulas. We are not going to do that, however, because while correlations found in population level statistics can be a clue for future scientific inquiry, their primary use on an individual level is as a weapon against disempowered groups, societal inequalities disguised by the neutral-sounding language of statistics.

Describing Frederick L. Hoffman's pioneering 1896 work of actuarial science, Race Traits and Tendencies of the America Negro, a product of his work for the Prudential Life Insurance Company, Megan Wolff, MPH, writes: "To sell insurance policies at equal rates or for equal benefits across racial lines would offend a predominant system of social beliefs about the worth of human lives. But at the same time, only a scientific, statistical explanation could be used in the age of progress to publicly explain Prudential's decision." (Source)

Therefore, even in this small project, we will not engage in this flawed--morally and scientifically--method. Please enter your own expected lifespan, based on your best guess.
